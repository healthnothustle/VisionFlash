How Vision Flash works

The methodology behind the method

The idea

Most vision boards work through conscious reinforcement — you see an image, you feel it, you return to it. Vision Flash works differently. By cycling images at increasing speeds, you move from conscious viewing into subliminal imprinting, where images register in your nervous system before the critical mind can evaluate or dismiss them.

How to use it

  1. 1

    Create themed folders — one intention per folder. Keep images emotionally resonant, not just aspirational.

  2. 2

    Load 10–30 images that represent the feeling of your intention already fulfilled.

  3. 3

    Start slow. Spend 1–2 minutes viewing each image consciously. Feel what they activate in you.

  4. 4

    Gradually increase speed until images blur. You're now feeding directly to your subconscious.

  5. 5

    Stay in a receptive state — soft gaze, open breath. Don't try to see the images. Let them land.

  6. 6

    Slow back down and let one final image settle. Hold it. Return to your day.

A note on images

Choose images that evoke feeling, not just things. A photo of a woman laughing freely is more powerful than a photo of a sports car. The nervous system responds to emotional content, not objects.
